Flow with Purpose

In the heart of Wisconsin's rivers—Waupaca, Waushara, Portage—we turn properties into partnerships. Since 2012, REPCO has been more than real estate: a cooperative vision where every deal lifts families, sustains communities, and envisions a future of shared prosperity.

Our Visionary Roots

Born in 2012 from the lessons of economic tides, REPCO was founded by kind-hearted visionaries who saw real estate not as transactions, but as lifelines. In Waupaca's welcoming waters, we began helping neighbors navigate foreclosures, tax burdens, and inheritances—before banks could dam the flow. Today, as a true cooperative, we pool expertise to acquire, revitalize, and redistribute properties ethically, always prioritizing people over profit. Our mantra: Properties should nourish, like the rivers that carve our landscapes.

"Decent humans making smart waves." – REPCO Founders

Our Cooperative Pathways

Commercial Havens

Acquire and rent vibrant spaces that anchor communities—quick, fair closes for owners ready to pass the torch.

Home Renewals

Rescue homes via sheriff sales, breathe new life through thoughtful remodels, and resell to families seeking stability.

Land Legacies

Subdivide large parcels sustainably, unlocking value while preserving Wisconsin's green veins for generations.

Investment Streams

Secure rental properties that provide steady flow—empowering tenants and investors in equal measure.

Outreach Rivers: Connecting Communities

Like the Waupaca River's gentle curve, our outreach flows through three vital counties—Waupaca (the heart), Waushara (the wild tributary), and Portage (the steady current). We monitor sheriff sales, reach tax-delinquent owners with empathy, and scout divisible lands, always offering bridges: free consults, resource guides, and cooperative buys that protect credit and dreams.

A Family's Turn

In Waushara, we helped the Johnsons split their 20-acre parcel—retaining their homestead while funding their kids' future. "REPCO didn't just buy land; they restored our flow."

Foreclosure Bridge

Pre-auction in Portage, a quick cash offer saved the Garcias from credit ruin. Now, their home rents affordably, circling good back into the community.
